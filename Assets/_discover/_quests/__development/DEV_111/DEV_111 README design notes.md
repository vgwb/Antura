## Game Design Notes
Travel to the marble quarries of Carrara, high in the Apuan Alps of Tuscany, and help Francesco fix the extraction machine that Antura accidentally broke. Along the way, meet the spirits of three great artists — Nicola Pisano, Michelangelo, and Bernini — and discover how the most famous marble in the world has shaped art for over 2,000 years.
**Mission Objective**
The player joins a school field trip to the marble quarries of Carrara. Antura has broken the extraction machine that Francesco the quarry worker was about to demonstrate. Three missing mechanical pieces have fallen somewhere in the quarry. The player must explore the caves, meet the spirits of three famous artists, complete a challenge for each one, and recover the pieces — before finally watching the machine cut a block of marble for the very first time.

**Characters**
Francesco
A proud and experienced quarry worker who was about to demonstrate the extraction machine when Antura broke it. He opens and closes the quest, gives the player their mission, and reassembles the machine at the end. Pragmatic and warm, slightly exasperated by Antura.

Spirit of Nicola Pisano
The ghost of the medieval sculptor who arrived in Carrara in 1260 to choose marble for the Baptistery of Pisa. Solemn and formal, he speaks like a teacher. He explains all three extraction techniques before testing the player with a matching activity.

Spirit of Michelangelo
The ghost of Michelangelo Buonarroti, passionate and demanding. He spent eight months in these quarries choosing marble for the Tomb of Julius II. He explains the lizzatura and his philosophy of liberation — the sculpture is already inside the block. He tests the player through direct conversation rather than a mini-game.

Spirit of Bernini
The ghost of Gian Lorenzo Bernini, theatrical and enthusiastic. He carved Apollo and Daphne at only twenty-three years old. He contrasts the slow work of chisels and mallets in his era with the speed of the modern diamond wire, before challenging the player with a jigsaw of his sculpture.

Antura
The dog who accidentally broke the extraction machine while playing. He appears briefly at the start of the quest, ashamed, before disappearing. His mistake is the engine that drives the whole adventure.


### Learning Goals
Extraction Techniques: To understand that marble has been extracted for over 2,000 years using three very different methods — the Roman wooden wedge, gunpowder explosives (1600s), and the modern diamond wire (1970s onward).
The Lizzatura: To learn that marble blocks — weighing as much as ten cars — were once transported down steep mountain slopes on wooden sleds called lizze, controlled by thick hemp ropes. A dangerous and spectacular job.
Nicola Pisano (1260): To recognise Nicola Pisano as the first great Italian sculptor to use Carrara marble, for the pulpit of the Baptistery of Pisa.
Michelangelo (1497–1520): To know that Michelangelo personally spent months in Carrara selecting marble blocks, and used them for the Pietà, the David, and the Moses. His philosophy: “The sculpture is already inside the block — I just free it.”
Bernini (1622–1625): To discover that Bernini carved Apollo and Daphne from Carrara marble at age 23, making the stone look soft as skin and light as leaves — now at the Galleria Borghese in Rome.
Carrara Today: To know that Carrara has 165 active quarries, the marble is shipped worldwide from its port, and UNESCO named it a Creative City for marble craftsmanship in 2017

### Gameplay Structure
The Mission
The quest opens at the entrance plaza of the Carrara quarry. Francesco stands proudly next to the extraction machine, ready to give a demonstration. The sign at the entrance reads: “Welcome to the Carrara Marble Quarries — open for over 2,000 years.”

Francesco:
“Hi! I’m Francesco, a quarry worker. Today I’ll show you how marble is extracted!
Look at this machine — it’s incredibly powerful. Let me start it up...
Wait… it’s not working! Something is wrong.
Antura! Was it you? Look what you’ve done!
Three parts of the machine have fallen into the quarry.
You need to find them. Maybe the spirits of the quarry will help you.”


Three glowing spirit markers appear on the map. The player can visit them in any order.

First Spirit: Nicola Pisano
Location: Medieval zone — stone walls with ancient carved markings

Nicola Pisano:
“I am Nicola Pisano, medieval sculptor. It was 1260 when I first came here.
I chose this marble for the Baptistery of Pisa — but first, let me tell you how it’s extracted.

First technique: the wooden wedge. The Romans inserted fig-wood wedges into cracks in the rock.
Then they soaked the wood — as it swelled, it split the stone. Clever, wasn’t it?

Second technique: explosives. From 1600 onwards, workers used gunpowder.
They blasted the mountainside to break off blocks. Fast, but it destroyed a lot of marble.

Third technique: the diamond wire. Since 1970, a steel wire coated in diamonds.
It cuts with perfect precision. In one day you can cut what once took three months!
Now — can you match each technique to its era?”


Activity: Match each technique (wooden wedge / explosives / diamond wire) to its correct historical period.
Reward: Machine piece #1.

Second Spirit: Michelangelo
Location: High zone of the quarry — enormous blocks, majestic atmosphere

Michelangelo:
“I am Michelangelo Buonarroti. Perhaps the greatest sculptor who ever lived.
I came to Carrara in person to choose my blocks — I trusted no one else.
I spent eight months here just for the Tomb of Julius II.

Marble is not just a rock. The sculpture is already inside the block.
My work is to remove the excess marble and set it free.
With this marble I carved the Pietà, the David, and the Moses.

But do you know how the blocks reached Florence or Rome?
They weigh as much as ten cars — and there were no trucks, no trains.
Workers slid them down the mountain on wooden sleds called lizze.
Thick hemp ropes controlled the speed. The most dangerous job that existed.
Many quarrymen lost their lives.

Now tell me — what do you call the sled they used to carry the blocks?”
  → The lizza.     [CORRECT] “Exactly. A dangerous and spectacular method.”
  → The carriage.  [WRONG]   “No — there were no roads. It was a wooden sled, called a lizza.”

Michelangelo: “And which artwork did I carve from Carrara marble?”
  → The Pietà.    [CORRECT] “Yes. And the David. And the Moses. All from this stone.”
  → The Last Supper.  [WRONG]   “That is a painting by Leonardo! I work in marble.”


Interaction: Dialogue with two choice questions. No mini-game — Michelangelo tests the player through conversation.
Reward: Machine piece #2.

Third Spirit: Bernini
Location: Open bright area — large freshly cut blocks

Bernini:
“I am Gian Lorenzo Bernini! Sculptor, architect, painter.
I carved Apollo and Daphne from Carrara marble — between 1622 and 1625.
I was only twenty-three years old when I began!

In that sculpture you can see the exact moment Daphne transforms into a laurel tree.
Her fingers become branches, her feet roots, her skin bark.
All of this in marble — a rock as hard as iron. No one had dared attempt such a thing.

In my time, marble was cut with chisels and mallets. Every millimetre was sweat and effort.
Today the diamond wire runs at hundreds of metres per minute.
In a single day you do the work that in my time took three months.
I wish I had had such a tool! But now — put my sculpture back together!”


Activity: Jigsaw — reassemble 4 fragments of Apollo and Daphne.
Reward: Machine piece #3.

Back to Francesco
Location: Quarry plaza — machine repaired

The player returns with all three pieces. Francesco reassembles the machine.

Francesco:
“You found all the pieces! Thank you so much!
There we go… it works! Can you hear the wire?
Today Carrara has 165 active quarries. The marble ships worldwide from our port.
In 2017 UNESCO named us a Creative City for marble.

Tell me — what’s the technique we use today to cut the marble?”
  → The diamond wire.   [CORRECT] “Exactly! It cuts like butter.”
  → Explosives.            [WRONG]   “We used that once — it destroyed too much marble.”


Final Quiz
A 3-question quiz appears after Francesco’s dialogue:
Where does Carrara marble come from? → The Apuan Alps, Tuscany.
What is the name of the wooden sled used to carry blocks downhill? → The lizza.
Who carved the Pietà from Carrara marble? → Michelangelo.

Conclusion

Francesco:
“Bravo! You’ve learned so much today.
You know how marble was cut by the Romans, how Michelangelo chose his blocks,
and how Bernini made stone look soft as skin.
This marble built some of the most beautiful art in the world —
and ist all starts right here, in these mountains.”
