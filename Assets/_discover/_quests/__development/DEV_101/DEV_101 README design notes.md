## Game Design Notes
**Le Basi della Musica con Guido d'Arezzo**

## Basic Information
| | |
|---|---|
| **Title** | Le Basi della Musica |
| **Location** | Arezzo, Tuscany, Italy |
| **Theme** | Basic Music Theory |
| **Scenario** | Mixed (realistic city of Arezzo + narrative elements) |

## Overview
Antura has scattered the pages of the choir's music sheet all over Arezzo. The player must help the monk Guido d'Arezzo recover all the fragments and bring them back to the choir of the Cathedral of Saints Donato and Pietro so they can sing again.

## Mission Objective
Explore the city of Arezzo and collect all the scattered music sheet pages. Interact with NPCs, solve riddles and mini-games to recover the notes, then bring them back to the choir in the correct order.

## Characters

### Guido d'Arezzo
In the quest he accompanies the player throughout the city, explaining music theory concepts each time a note is found, and providing information about the historical places of Arezzo.

### Cantore
Chief of the choir of the Cathedral. He waits at the Duomo and receives the music sheet once all pages have been collected. He asks the player to reorder the notes before the choir can sing again.

## Learning Goals

### The player will understand:
- Music is written on an organized space called the **Pentagram** (staff)
- Notes have precise names: **Do, Re, Mi, Fa, Sol, La, Si**
- The **Treble Clef** provides a fixed reference point at the beginning of the staff

### The player will be able to:
- Distinguish between high and low notes graphically on the staff
- Recognize the symbol of the Treble Clef (Chiave di Sol)
- Associate the note names Do–Re–Mi–Fa–Sol–La–Si with their positions on the staff

| Re | Second note, written in the first space below the first line |
| Mi | Third note, written on the first line |
| Fa | Fourth note, written in the second space |
| Sol | Fifth note, written on the second line |

## Gameplay Structure

| Phase | Description |
|---|---|
| **Introduction** | The player meets Guido d'Arezzo in Piazza Guido Monaco. Antura has scattered the music sheet pages across the city. Guido asks for help and joins the player's party. |
| **Exploration** | The player freely explores the center of Arezzo, collecting the 9 music sheet pages scattered across historical locations. Some pages are found directly in the scene; others are unlocked by completing mini-games or solving riddles with NPCs. |
| **Last Task** | The player brings all pages to the Cantore at the Duomo. The pages are out of order — the player must complete a reorder activity to arrange them correctly (Do Re Mi Fa Sol La Si). |
| **Ending** | The choir sings again. Guido reminds the player that every song in the world is made of just these seven notes. |

## Activities
| Activity | Description |
|---|---|
| **Canvas** | Used to unlock individual notes (Do, Re, Mi, Fa, Sol, La, Si). Difficulty scales dynamically based on the player's progress. |
| **Riddle** | At Casa del Vasari, a tourist asks the player to identify the Treble Clef symbol. Unlocks the Sol page. |
| **Reorder** | Final activity at the Duomo. The player must arrange all collected pages in the correct order: Do Re Mi Fa Sol La Si. |
